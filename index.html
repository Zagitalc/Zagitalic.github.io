Causes of climate Change


Causes can be categorised into Human activities and natural carbon emisstions give rise to greenhouse gases such as CO2, Chlorofluorocarbons, and Methane, concentrated in the atmosphere, which might lead to global warming.

Human activities: Burning Fossil Fuels (coal and oil) to generate electricity

Since coal powerplants in the USA contribute toa 1/3 of elctricity , the process of Fuel Combustion causes CO2 and Methane to became major sources of greenhouse gas to the atmosphere

Human activities: Human Deforestation

Cutting down of trees can relase up to 1/5 of CO2 gas to the atmosphere, According to the Rainforest Alliance, 4/5 of which is due to Human Agricultural development

Human activities: Overconsumption of meat

Thanks to the special digestive system of Ruminants (cows and sheep),greenhouse gas methane is released which has 30 times the amount of CO2. That is 16% of Global anthropogenic co2 emission

Naturally occured causes:

Leakage of greenhouse gas from the Permafrost (frozen soil).

Permafrost in the Earth's crust accounts of up to 1400 gigatons of Carbon

Melting of such continent may lead to a rise in water level as well as intense carbon emission.


Evidence of climate Change: Tropical cyclones

Human activities and natural carbon emisstions give rise to greenhouse gases such as CO2, Chlorofluorocarbons, and Methane, concentrated in the atmosphere, which might lead to global warming. As a result, rising temperature give rise to extreme weathers. One of which is the formation of tropical storms called Hurricanes or Typhoons.

Intensity of hurricanes

Study from The Nature has also shown that the endurance of hurricanes have increased up to 20% from the 1960s, meaning an increase of longetivity and destruction to citizens of affected areas.

Precipitation caused by hurricanes

Study from The GDFL(Global Warming and Hurricanes) has also shown that the amount of precipitation has increased of 10-15% amist 2 degree Celsius of ocean temperature increase

Frequency of hurricanes

Study from PNAS has also shown that since the past 40 years, the frequency of hurricanes has an annual increase of up to 49% in the North Atlantic, Year 2020 is also the year of the most Atlantic hurricanes with up to 30 storms
