Causes of climate Change


Causes can be categorised into Human activities, including fossil fuel combustion, deforestation, consumption of meat, and naturally occured carbon emissions such as melting of permafrost.

Human activities: Burning Fossil Fuels (coal and oil) to generate electricity

Data shows coal powerplants in the USA contribute toa 1/3 of elctricity , the process of Fuel Combustion causes CO2 and Methane to became major sources of greenhouse gas to the atmosphere

Human activities: Human Deforestation

Cutting down of trees can relase up to 1/5 of CO2 gas to the atmosphere, According to the Rainforest Alliance, 4/5 of which is due to Human Agricultural development

Human activities: Overconsumption of meat

Thanks to the special digestive system of Ruminants (cows and sheep),greenhouse gas methane is released which has 30 times the amount of CO2. That is 16% of Global anthropogenic co2 emission

Naturally occured causes:

Leakage of greenhouse gas from the Permafrost (frozen soil).

Permafrost in the Earth's crust accounts of up to 1400 gigatons of Carbon

Melting of such continent may lead to a rise in water level as well as intense carbon emission.


Evidence of climate Change: Tropical cyclones

Human activities and natural carbon emisstions give rise to greenhouse gases such as CO2, Chlorofluorocarbons, and Methane, concentrated in the atmosphere, which might lead to global warming. As a result, rising temperature give rise to extreme weathers. One of which is the formation of tropical storms called Hurricanes or Typhoons.

Intensity of hurricanes

Study from The Nature has also shown that According to Li (2020), the endurance of hurricanes have increased up to 20% from the 1960s, meaning an increase of longetivity and destruction to citizens of affected areas.

Precipitation caused by hurricanes

Study from The GDFL(Global Warming and Hurricanes) has also shown that the amount of precipitation has increased of 10-15% amist 2 degree Celsius of ocean temperature increase

Frequency of hurricanes

Study from PNAS has also shown that since the past 40 years, the frequency of hurricanes has an annual increase of up to 49% in the North Atlantic, Year 2020 is also the year of the most Atlantic hurricanes with up to 30 storms

According to Climate dynamics(2013) and Li (2020), an increasing trend between rise of ocean temperature and global warming has been observed. The data however, fails to show such trend. The first two graphs show the frequency of Hurricanes and over 100mph windspeed hurricanes in every 10 year period. However these 2 graphs could not be representative of the trend due to variation of datapoints. In both cases, there is a general increasing trend of hurricanes from the 1850s to 1950s, a strong decline in the 1960s and another increase from that year onwards. However, given the drastic increase of sea temperature with time didn't reflect the increasing trend of hurricanes, such a relationship could not be deduced.

The third graph shows the percentage of stronger hurricanes in all storms, which could more accuractely describe whether or not the relation of storms and global warming exists. There are still fluctuations throughtout various decades. The trend reaches a maximum of 38.5% of 100mph hurricanes in 1960-69 period, however, plunges to new low of 18.8% in the 1960-69 season. The trend rose slightly throughout the rest of the seasons but remains a steady average of 25 cyclones per decade in 1940-49 season remains the most frequent of the group with 10 hurricanes. As a result, it is insignificant to call an increasing trend between frequency of tropical storms and time due to the rise in sea temperature.
